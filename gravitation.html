Gravitation

Every mass pulls on every other mass. That single idea holds you to the floor, keeps the Moon circling Earth, and keeps Earth circling the Sun. Play with each simulation below — drag things, drop things, change the sliders.

01 — Orbits

Planets orbiting the Sun

A planet keeps falling toward the Sun but keeps missing it — that endless "falling and missing" is an orbit.

Try it

Speed up time to see the inner planets lap the outer ones. Notice how a planet closer to the Sun moves faster — gravity is stronger there.

Bonus · Extra knowledge

Einstein's spacetime fabric

This is beyond Class 9 syllabus, but it's the deeper picture: Einstein described gravity not as a pull, but as mass bending the fabric of space itself. Nearby objects simply roll along the curve.

Try it

Drag the heavy ball around the grid. Notice how it dents the fabric — and how the small ball orbits by rolling along that dent, not by being "pulled" in the usual sense.
